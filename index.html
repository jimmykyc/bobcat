About BOBCAT

“Bobcat” is the codename for a low-power processor architecture designed by AMD (Advanced Micro Devices). The Bobcat architecture was first introduced in 2011 and is used in AMD’s line of APUs (Accelerated Processing Units), which combine both CPU (Central Processing Unit) and GPU (Graphics Processing Unit) capabilities on a single chip. The Bobcat architecture is designed for use in small, portable devices such as netbooks, laptops, and tablets, where power efficiency and low heat generation are important considerations.
